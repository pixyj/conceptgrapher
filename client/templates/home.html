Welcome to ConceptGrapher!

This app was born when I noticed that tutorials and documentations on the web don't really assess whether you've mastered the material.
Also, when you browse a site like Wikipedia to learn about a particular concept, you end up opening multiple articles that usually aren't necessary to understand the concept.

ConceptGrapher complements these resources by assessing your existing skills in a particular topic and then helping you navigate through the tutorial by checking your answers to quizzes.

How does it work?

ConceptGrapher works by breaking down a particular topic into atomic concepts and creating a dependency graph among the concepts.
Let's take python as an example. You need to understand what a function does before learning about keyword arguments. So function is a prerequisite to keyword arguments.

Similar relationships are created among other concepts in the topic. Finally, the concepts are sorted topologically to navigate you through the tutorial in the right order.
